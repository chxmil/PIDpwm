## Issue 3 — Saturated Sensor Silent Failure (Python-side guard)

- **Status:** Open — fix designed but not yet merged.
- **Severity:** Medium — Issue 8 demonstrates Stage-2 is landing on the clamp ceiling silently.
- **Filed:** 2026-05-08
- **Last touched:** 2026-05-11

### Summary
When ≥ 90% of Stage-2 calibration samples are at the 800 kΩ clamp ceiling, abort the grip with a clear warning instead of silently proceeding with a corrupt baseline.

### Proposed fix (~10 min including verification)
```python
sat_count = sum(1 for r in res_samples if r >= 799.99)
if res_samples and sat_count >= len(res_samples) * 0.9:
    print(f"  ❌ SENSOR SATURATED — {sat_count}/{len(res_samples)} samples at 800 kOhm clamp.")
    print("     Likely causes: tactile sensor disconnected, broken wires,")
    print("     ADS1115 acquisition issue (see Issue 7/8), ADC reading near rail.")
    print("     Aborting grip. Fix the sensor and try again.")
    ser.write("PWM:0")
    return 0
```

- **B.** Per-packet logging when `res_k > 800` is clamped (warn at end of grip if > 50% clamped).
- **C.** Make the initial verify check abort or pause instead of warning-and-continuing.

## Issue 8 — Stage-2 Baseline Calibration Clamping at 800 kΩ

- **Status:** Open
- **Severity:** Medium — silently corrupts `res_drop_pct` feature; no direct effect on force PID.
- **Filed:** 2026-05-11
- **Affected files:** `ModelInclude.py::run_one_grip()` Stage 2; clamp ceiling constant; potentially `Code Store/PIDpwmClaude/PIDpwmClaude.ino`

### Summary
`baseline_res_k` reads 800.0000 on most trials. Per-packet resistance is healthy (400 kΩ–2.3 MΩ) — Issue 7's firmware fix held at the per-packet level. The downstream problem is that healthy idle resistance straddles the 800 kΩ Python clamp ceiling.

### CNN-PID v2 evidence (2026-05-13)
CNN-PID v2's `res_norm` channel is also affected (it normalises by the saturated baseline), yet the CNN still scores 0.942. This suggests `res_norm` contributes less than the other four channels (`shifted_cond`, `delta_pos`, derivative pair) which don't depend on the saturated baseline — and that a clamp-ceiling fix would only marginally improve CNN-PID, not RF where `res_drop_pct` has importance 0.125.

### Recommended next step
1. Spot-check `data_logs/datasets/NewModel (1).csv` for adc1 bimodality.
2. Multimeter on AIN1-to-GND during a true-idle Stage-2; if R is 800 kΩ–1.5 MΩ matching multimeter, bump the clamp ceiling to 2000 kΩ.
3. Land Issue 3's saturation guard.
4. Consider widening Stage-2 to 60 samples with 10% trim.

### Acceptance criteria
- `baseline_res_k` in 400–1500 kΩ range on ≥ 80% of trials in a labelled session, AND
- `res_drop_pct` regains a meaningful spread.

## Issue 9 — Hard/Medium Feature Overlap Under PID Overshoot

- **Status:** Open — **partially mitigated** by CNN-PID v2.
- **Severity:** Medium (downgraded from Medium-high) — CNN-PID reduces the error count materially but the structural cause persists.
- **Filed:** 2026-05-11
- **Couples to:** Issue 2 (Force Tracking Variance) — Issue 9 is its downstream classification cost.
- **Last touched:** 2026-05-13 (CNN-PID v2 evidence)

### Summary
Training and field `f_peak` distributions for Hard and Medium overlap almost completely. The signal-side problem persists. What changed today is the *modelling side*: a CNN that uses time-series shape (instead of scalar `f_peak`) can extract more from the same overlapping data.

### CNN-PID v2 evidence (2026-05-13)
| | RF v4 (5-fold CV) | CNN-PID v2 (5-fold CV) |
|---|---|---|
| Hard recall | 0.757 | **0.911** |
| Medium recall | 0.824 | **0.946** |
| Hard↔Medium misclassifications | 24 of 35 | 10 of 15 |

The discriminative information is in the trajectory **shape** (slopes, response timings), not the peak — RF's hand-crafted scalars throw most of it away. The CNN keeps the (40, 5) signature.

In-sample back-test (260 trials in `data_logs/datasets/`): CNN-PID strictly dominates RF — 11/0 disagreement asymmetry, no case where RF is right and CNN is wrong.

### Three solution paths (status 2026-05-13)
- **Path A — Fix the PID.** Re-tuned 2026-05-12 (`KP=70, KI=20, KD=7, TARGET_FORCE=3.5`); verification pending. Would benefit both RF and CNN-PID.
- **Path B — New feature set.** CNN-PID v2 *is* this path, executed: time-series features replacing peak-anchored scalars. Beats RF by 0.104 CV.
- **Path C — Accept the limit.** Document as the article's known limitation. Less urgent now that CNN-PID has bought back most of the gap.

### Acceptance criteria (updated)
On a labelled 3-class field session:
- Hard recall ≥ 0.70 AND Medium recall ≥ 0.70 AND Soft recall ≥ 0.90, AND
- Field A/B confirms CNN-PID v2's CV gap (+0.104) holds out-of-distribution.
